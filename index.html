FAQ's

Why choose a freelance web designer over an agency?

You will be dealing with one individual from start to finish. Therefore the cost of your project will be far more affordable without compromising on quality. There will be no need to go through a support team to get something done!

What do I need to run a website?

You will need a web hosting provider and a registered domain name. If you are unsure how to go about this I can talk you through the process.
In essence, the web hosting provides you with an online space on the World Wide Web and the domain name (a.k.a. URL) is the path to that space (eg www.ladycornish.com). They can (and should) be bought from and registered with the same provider. They are prerequisites to run a website and must be in place as soon as the project moves from the design phase in to development. *

The hosting package is one of the main factors for website speed so it’s very important to choose wisely. I am happy to walk you through this process. There are quite a few options out there but I can personally recommend IONOS (1AND1). I have purchased several domain names and hosted several websites with them for many years, business & personal sites. If you would like to check them out please click here or contact me for details.

* Web hosting and domain registration fees are paid directly to the chosen provider.

How much does a new website cost?

The honest answer here is - it depends. It depends on the style of website you’re looking for as they will require different types of functionality (informational, online shop, portfolio, etc). However, I aim to (as far as possible) ensure everyone can afford a website, so please just get in touch!
An ideal way to get a quote is by sending me an email or a message explaining what you have in mind and want to achieve with your project. In most cases I'm able to provide an estimate straight away.

How long does a new website take to be completed?

The answer is virtually the same as for the question above. To give you an idea, very basic websites can take between 4 to 6 weeks and more complex ones 8 to 12 weeks. However this is just an average time frame, some may be quicker. It really does depend on what you have requested.

What about a website refresh?

If you are looking to refresh an existing website, the ideal way of getting a quote and time frame is by sending me a "list of changes" you want to implement. Provide me with your website address and tell me what you like about your site and what you would like improved.
